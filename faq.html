Q. What is the connection between poverty and global citizenship?
A. Poverty and global citizenship are interconnected as global citizens recognize the shared responsibility to address and alleviate poverty on a global scale.
Q. How does poverty impact different regions globally?
A. Poverty impacts different regions globally by exacerbating social inequalities, limiting access to education and healthcare, and hindering economic development in vulnerable communities.

A. Individuals can contribute to alleviating poverty globally by supporting charitable organizations, raising awareness, advocating for fair trade practices, and making sustainable consumer choices.

A. Globalization influences poverty dynamics by creating opportunities for economic growth but also contributing to income inequality, exploitation, and environmental degradation in certain regions.

A. Businesses and corporations can promote social responsibility in the context of global poverty by implementing ethical business practices, supporting local communities, and investing in sustainable development projects.

A. Key factors contributing to poverty as a global citizenship issue include unequal distribution of resources, lack of access to education and healthcare, political instability, and systemic economic injustices.
